Developmental Milestones for Babies by Age

The skills your child learns as she grows are called developmental milestones. Here's what to expect during the first two years.

Milestones by age

1-month-old

Sleeping 14 to 17 hours each day, four or five daytime naps.

About eight feedings per day for breastfed babies.

Your baby is becoming more aware of you, and may turn to you when they hear your voice.

Turns head from side to side, gets excited by familiar faces.

2-month-old

Your baby is generally more communicative now, they may be starting to smile when they see you and will be starting to “coo.”

Recognize faces of parents.

Your baby is not sleeping through the night yet, but may be sleeping longer stretches at night.

The posterior fontanelle closes.

They should be able to lift their head 45 degrees when you place them on their tummy.

Your baby starts to learn to self-soothe by sucking on their hands or fingers, more purposefully brings their hands to their mouth

3-month-old

Your baby will start to recognize you from farther distances, and will now be able to start lifting their head and chest up from a belly-down position.

The length between feedings has stretched out and you may have a more predictable feeding routine.

More of your baby’s sleep happens at night now, and your baby may start sleeping longer stretches at night.

Smiles more, holds head at 90-degree angle, and bears weight on legs.

Finds hands, sucks thumbs and fingers, and clasps objets.

They will continue to expand and refine their vocal skills, cooing and even starting to babble.

4-month-old

Your baby's vision is improving, they are becoming more sociable, and are beginning to reach and roll.

Your baby is likely taking three to four naps a day. The dreaded 4-month sleep regression may make an appearance.

Should be able to push up onto their elbows or hands during tummy time. They may even start rolling from belly to back.

Reaches for objects with one hand, swings at dangling toys, smiles spontaneously.

Has grown big enough (at least doubled their birth weight)

5-month-old

Your baby is becoming more vocal (babbling, crying), smiling, reaching for objects, holding their head up, and rolling.

Your 5-month-old should be getting around 10 hours of sleep at night, with two to three naps during the day.

Your baby is still drinking formula or breastmilk (around 6-8 ounces, four to five times a day), and might be interested in solid foods.

5-month-olds start gaining the ability to hold their heads up without support, bear weight on their legs when stood up, push onto their elbows from their tummies, and may even push up into a sitting position.

Babies are typically rolling two different ways: tummy-to-back and back-to-tummy.

6-month-old

Your baby will likely lean on their hands to support themselves when sitting, babble, show more emotion, and be extremely curious about the world around them—often grabbing and putting things in their mouth.

This month, your baby will begin to consolidate their sleep and may even begin sleeping through the night as well as nap two to three times a day.

Even though you will likely introduce your baby to solids this month, most of your baby's nutritional needs will still be met by breastmilk or formula.

Likes to look in the mirror.

Rolls from front to back and back to front, sits without support, and tries to crawl.

7-month-old

Your baby continues to build on the skills they were learning at 6 months including being able to sit up, move around, and pick things up with a raking grasp.

At this age, your baby will continue to consolidate their sleep including sleeping more at night and taking about two naps a day, for a total of about 14 hours of sleep a day.

When your baby is 7 months old, breastmilk or formula is still their primary source of nutrition with some babies consuming as much as 32 ounces a day or nursing three to five times a day.

Your baby will continue to babble as they did at 6 months, but they also may make specific sounds tied to emotions like happiness or frustration.

Recognizes familiar faces.

8-month-old

At this age, babies are becoming very mobile. Not only are they moving in some way—possibly even crawling—they also are becoming more vocal making different vowel and consonant sounds as well as responding with inflection based on their mood.

Babies this age are sleeping around 14 hours total, with 10 to 11 of those hours occurring at night. They also are taking about two naps a day.

By the time your baby is 8 months old, they are probably nursing or taking a bottle about three to five times a day as well as eating a mixture of solid foods.

Picks up objects between the thumb and forefinger (pincer grasp).

Pulls into a standing position near furniture.

9-month-old

At 9 months, your baby is developing a number of skills like crawling, cruising, and the pincer grasp. They also are expanding their language and communication skills and can make a variety of noises as well as shake their head no and point to things.

On average, babies this age are sleeping a total of 14 hours a day with about 11 hours of sleep occurring at night and three hours split between two naps during the day.

While your baby is still nursing or taking a bottle, they are likely reducing the amount of formula or breastmilk they are consuming in favor of more solids. Most babies this age are eating about three meals a day and possibly having snacks too.

Waves "bye-bye" when prompted, loves opening and closing things.

Stands up unassisted, starts taking steps, even if holding onto something.

10-month-old

Your baby is becoming increasingly mobile, moving around their environment by crawling, cruising, and pulling to a stand. They also may be able to say words like "mama" and "dada" as well point, poke, and pick things up with their thumb and forefinger.

Babies this age are sleeping a total of about 14 hours a day with 11 of those hours at night and three hours during the day.Most babies are taking two naps a day, but some will be transitioning to one longer nap in the afternoon.

By now, your baby is probably a pro at finger foods and is able to feed themselves—although it may get a little messy at times.

Walks with you holding their hands, may take a step or two.

Drinks from a cup held for them.

11-month-old

Babies this age are not only developing gross motor skills like standing and walking, but they also are making strides in communication by saying one or two words and gesturing with their hands. They also are perfecting the pincer grasp and can throw objects.

Your baby needs about 14 hours of sleep, with about 11 of those hours occurring at night.

Babies this age are starting to reduce the amount of formula or breast milk that they are consuming in favor of solid foods.They should not be given cow's milk or alternative dairy-free milks until after their first birthday.

Babies also can learn to crawl up the stairs if they are allowed to practice with close supervision by the parent.

Finds toys when hidden under a cup, bounces to music.

12-month-old

Becomes more talkative. Responds to their name. Tries to repeat words you say

Takes first steps. Some babies begin to walk around 12 months old, while others wait a little longer (around 14 to 15 months).

Likes to place objects in and out of containers. Points with index finger. Drinks from an open cup if you hold it for them.

For the most part, 1-year-olds prefer to play next to other kids, as opposed to with them. This is called parallel play. But you may see your child begin to include other kids in their play at times.

When you leave the room for just a moment and your baby starts crying uncontrollably. Hello, separation anxiety! Your 1-year-old is very attached to you, and it is a normal part of their development.
